What makes these Maps unique

Valorant maps are different in many ways each map gives different situations and possibilities that can happen when you defuse the bomb. Some maps have different layouts, for example, Lotus and Haven both have 3 bomb sites: A, B, and C. Some maps offer other elements such as closing doors, teleporters for easy rotation, ziplines to get to tall spaces, some have more ultimate orbs than others, and even spawn locations. Another example is fraction where you are able to spawn in the middle of the map, then the usual opposite sides. All of these maps all have different elements that make the map more challenging for the players. Valorant also features map rotations in the competitive pool to keep the game fresh, allowing the competitive meta to remain balanced instead of becoming broken.

What clarifies as a Good map

What clarifies as a good map, personally, is that I like maps that are open and have a lot more room to breathe, rather than close-quarters maps that prevent you from staying in one place the whole time. This can make it predictable and allow other agents to have an advantage on certain maps. On certain maps, certain agents are able to have a big advantage over the other players, which can lead to changes being made around the map to make it more balanced for everyone in order to make the game more enjoyable and balanced. Maps that have been changed over the years have been Breeze, Bind, Ascent, etc.
